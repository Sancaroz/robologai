Glossary

Key terms for readers entering the robotics and AI economy.

Short definitions help new readers stay on the page instead of leaving to search every technical term.
Physical AIAI connected to sensors, robots, machines, and real-world action.
Embodied AIIntelligence placed inside a body that can perceive and move in physical space.
Humanoid RobotA robot shaped for human environments, usually with arms, legs, hands, and sensors.
TeleoperationRemote human control used to train, supervise, or operate robots.
RaaSRobotics as a Service: paying for robot usage instead of buying the robot outright.
SimulationVirtual training environments where robots can learn before real-world deployment.
End EffectorThe hand, gripper, or tool at the end of a robotic arm.
Autonomy StackThe software layers for perception, planning, control, and decision-making.
